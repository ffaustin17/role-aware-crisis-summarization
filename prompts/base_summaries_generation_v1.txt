You are generating synthetic target summaries for a research dataset on role-aware crisis communication.

Task:
Given a crisis-related tweet, its assigned responder role(s), and its disaster type, generate:
1. one short operational note for each assigned responder role
2. one final integrated role-aware summary for the tweet

Use only the information supported by the tweet. Do not invent casualties, locations, hazards, agencies, road closures, evacuations, or rescue details that are not stated or strongly implied.

Responder role guidance:
- Police: public safety, threat control, crowd control, evacuation support, traffic/access control, scene security, criminal activity.
- EMS: injuries, casualties, triage, medical urgency, patient transport, vulnerable victims, medical rescue support.
- Firefighter: fire spread, smoke, containment, hazardous materials, structural danger, trapped people, search and rescue.

Rules:
- The keys in "base_summaries" must exactly match the assigned responder roles.
- If there is one role, the final summary may match that role's note.
- If there are multiple roles, the final summary must integrate all assigned roles into one concise paragraph or sentence.
- Keep each role note under 25 words.
- Keep the final summary under 45 words.
- Use cautious language such as "reported", "possible", or "may require" when the tweet is uncertain.
- Do not include hashtags, URLs, usernames, or retweet markers unless essential.
- Output valid JSON only. Do not include markdown.

Input:
Responder Roles: {roles}
Disaster Type: {disaster_type}
Tweet: {tweet_text}

Output JSON format:
{
  "base_summaries": {
    "<RoleName>": "<short role-specific operational note>"
  },
  "final_base_summary_text": "<one integrated role-aware operational summary>",
  "quality_notes": "<brief note about faithfulness or uncertainty>"
}
