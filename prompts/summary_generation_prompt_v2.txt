You are generating synthetic target summaries for a research dataset on role-aware crisis communication.

Task:
Given a crisis-related tweet, responder role(s), disaster type, and optional FReCS metadata, generate:
1. one short operational note for each assigned responder role
2. one final integrated role-aware operational summary for the tweet
3. one structured quality note explaining evidence strength and assumptions

Important framing:
This is not a normal tweet summary task. Do not simply restate the tweet.
Your goal is to explain what each assigned responder role should operationally pay attention to based on the tweet and metadata.

Use the tweet as the primary evidence.
Use Disaster Type, Secondary Annotation, and Information Type as contextual guidance.
Do not let metadata override the tweet. If metadata suggests a role-specific concern but the tweet is vague, use cautious language.

Responder role guidance:
- Police: public safety, threat control, crowd control, evacuation support, traffic/access control, scene security, criminal activity.
- EMS: injuries, casualties, triage, medical urgency, patient transport, vulnerable victims, medical rescue support.
- Firefighter: fire spread, smoke, containment, hazardous materials, structural danger, trapped people, search and rescue.

Metadata guidance:
- Secondary Annotation indicates the specialized responder sub-unit or operational layer.
- Information Type indicates the kind of crisis information being communicated.
- Use these fields to make the operational note more specific when they are consistent with the tweet.
- Do not merely restate metadata labels.
- Do not invent active operations, confirmed casualties, road closures, evacuations, trapped victims, fires, crimes, or hazardous-material incidents unless stated or strongly implied.

FReCS secondary annotation context:
Police:
- DCC = Dispatch Call Center: supports emergency call intake, routing, coordination, and dispatch prioritization.
- TEU = Traffic Enforcement Unit: supports traffic control, access control, road safety, and movement around affected areas.
- CPU = Crime Protection / Prevention Unit: supports threat assessment, public safety, crime prevention, and scene security.

EMS:
- MMU = Mobile Medical Unit: supports field medical care, triage, stabilization, and patient transport.
- CERT = Community Emergency Response Team: supports community-level emergency assistance, light rescue, basic aid, and local coordination.

Firefighter:
- FC = Fire Control: supports fire suppression, containment, smoke/fire response, and fire spread prevention.
- HAZMAT = Hazardous Materials: supports hazardous exposure assessment, containment, contamination risk, and responder safety.
- USAR = Urban Search and Rescue: supports locating, reaching, and extracting trapped or endangered people in damaged structures or difficult environments.

Rules:
- The keys in "base_summaries" must exactly match the assigned responder roles.
- If there is one role, the final summary may match that role's note.
- If there are multiple roles, the final summary must integrate all assigned roles into one concise paragraph or sentence.
- Keep each role note under 25 words.
- Keep the final summary under 45 words.
- Prefer concrete operational implications over generic phrases like "ensure public safety" or "monitor the situation."
- Use cautious language such as "reported", "possible", "may require", or "should assess" when the tweet is uncertain.
- Avoid overusing "may require" in every role note; vary cautious phrasing naturally.
- Do not include hashtags, URLs, usernames, or retweet markers unless essential.
- Output valid JSON only. Do not include markdown.

Input:
Responder Roles: {roles}
Disaster Type: {disaster_type}
Tweet: {tweet_text}
Annotation: {secondary_annotations}
Information Type: {information_type}

Output JSON format:
{
  "base_summaries": {
    "<RoleName>": "<short role-specific operational note>"
  },
  "final_base_summary_text": "<one integrated role-aware operational summary>",
  "quality_notes": {
    "evidence_strength": "High | Medium | Low",
    "supporting_signals": ["Tweet", "Disaster Type", "Secondary Annotation", "Information Type"],
    "assumptions": ["<brief assumption or 'None'>"],
    "faithfulness_note": "<brief note about uncertainty, grounding, or metadata overreach risk>"
  }
}
